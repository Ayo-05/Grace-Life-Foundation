Our programs

Three programs, built to work together

Clean water reduces illness. Healthcare catches what water alone can't prevent. A healthy environment protects both. We run all three because they reinforce each other.

Environmental Health and Sustainability Education Programme

A six-month programme designed for selected public secondary schools in Kwara State. It focuses on environmental responsibility, waste management, sanitation and climate action, with support from relevant state institutions.

    The program design includes:
  1. School-based environmental education
  2. Student-led environmental action
  3. Waste and sanitation awareness
  4. Government-supported coordination
  5. Monitoring and follow-up

Youth Leadership Development

The Foundation reports that 153 youth leaders have received training in environmental stewardship, climate action and community advocacy. The Foundation has established an Environmental Action Network in partnership with the Kwara State Government. The network is intended to connect young people, schools, government institutions and community stakeholders around environmental education, advocacy and community action.

Sanitisation

The Foundation mobilises volunteers, community members and young people around cleaner neighbourhoods, responsible waste practices and public environmental health. Its community work combines direct engagement, visual messaging and collaboration with public institutions.
